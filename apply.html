Eligibility

Who Can Apply?

✅ Eligible Applicants

  • ▸ PhD students, postdoctoral researchers, and early-career researchers (ECRs) who received their PhD no more than 10 years ago
  • ▸ Researchers motivated to collaborate across disciplines and career stages
  • ▸ Researchers committed to open science and FAIR outputs
  • ▸ Able to attend both workshop days in Santiago, July 4–5, plus pre-event Zoom call (May 18–19)
  • ▸ Sufficient technical capacity to analyse datasets, or strong willingness to develop these skills

✗ Not Eligible

  • ▸ Master's students
  • ▸ Undergraduate students
Selection

Selection Process

Applications are reviewed in two stages. We are looking for a pool of participants that collectively represent a broad range of skills.

Stage 1

Initial Screen

Confirms attendance availability, career stage, years since PhD commencement, and volume of open outputs relative to time in academia.

Stage 2

Further Review

  • ▸ Commitment to open research outputs, given career stage
  • ▸ Evidence of collaborative research experience or willingness for team-based science
  • ▸ Demonstrated technical, analytical, or theoretical capacity relevant to working with open datasets
Prizes

The Consciousness Commons Prizes

Participating teams are eligible to compete for $25,000 in total seed funding to support continuation of their preregistered projects after the event.

🏆 Main Research Prize

Recognises projects with strong scientific merit, rigorous and transparent methodology, and effective cross-disciplinary collaboration.

🔓 Open Science Award

Recognises excellence in the implementation of open science practices, FAIR data reuse, and reproducible research workflows.

🤝 Collaboration Award

Recognises the development of particularly effective collaborative teams across institutions and disciplines.

Prize Eligibility Requirements

  • ▸ Form or join a Mind-Matched team during the pre-event phase
  • ▸ Submit a formal OSF preregistration during or within one week of the workshop
  • ▸ Demonstrate FAIR data reuse (PIDs, ORCIDs, open-source code with registered DOI)
  • ▸ Base the project on an openly available dataset at time of submission
  • ▸ Have at least one PI representative based at a research institution that can receive grant funding (PI must hold a PhD; funding cannot be awarded to institutions under Canadian or US sanctions)
